# COMPREHENSIVE PROJECT LOG: ISRO HACKATHON
## 6-Pollutant AQI Prediction

================================================================================
1. INTRODUCTION
================================================================================
Project Objective: Develop an AI/ML model for predicting the 6-pollutant Air Quality Index (AQI) (PM2.5, PM10, NO2, SO2, O3, CO) at a 0.25° grid over India.
Data Sources: Sentinel-5P (NO2, SO2, CO, AOD), INSAT-3D (AOD used as backup or complementary), and ERA5 meteorological reanalysis data.
Overall Architecture: A hybrid model utilizing a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) for spatial feature extraction from satellite images, coupled with an XGBoost residual corrector utilizing Global Average Pooling (GAP) embeddings and tabular meteorological/temporal features.
Geographic Block Split Rationale: To strictly prevent spatial data leakage, the dataset was split geographically rather than randomly. Training covers the North, West, and Central regions (accounting for 80% of data), while Validation encompasses the South and East/Northeast regions (20%). This ensures the model learns robust physical relationships rather than memorizing spatial proximity.

================================================================================
2. CHRONOLOGICAL PHASE LOG
================================================================================

### PHASE 1 — PATCH EXTRACTION (Sentinel-5P, INSAT-3D, ERA5)
**A. Purpose:** Extract 13x13 spatial patches centered around each CPCB station from satellite raster data to serve as CNN inputs.
**B. Implementation:**
- Regridded all Sentinel-5P .tif files to a unified 0.25° ERA5 grid matching the grid_spec.json (120x124 dimension).
- Extracted channels: NO2, SO2, CO, AOD.
- Extracted 13x13 patches for each station per day.
- Handled missing days robustly: CO missing on 2024-01-09, AOD missing on 2023-11-30.
**C. Challenges:** Aligning multiple satellite resolutions (Sentinel-5P vs ERA5) into a unified grid format.
**D. Debugging:** Verified grid_spec.json alignment to ensure North-to-South row consistency.
**E. Outcomes:**
- Total Training Patches: 8776
- Total Validation Patches: 2157
- Final Patch Shape: (N, 13, 13, 9)
**F. Output Files:**
- cnn_patches/X_image_train.npy
- cnn_patches/X_image_val.npy
- cnn_patches/metadata_train.csv
- cnn_patches/metadata_val.csv

### PHASE 2 — IMAGE QUALITY CONTROL & PREPROCESSING
**A. Purpose:** Clean the extracted patches by filtering out excessively cloudy/NaN-heavy images and imputing missing pixels.
**B. Implementation:**
- Implemented a 30% NaN threshold (patches with >30% missing pixels were dropped).
- Applied spatial median fill using a 3x3 `generic_filter` for isolated missing pixels.
- Applied a 2D median filter (`medfilt2d`, 3x3) exclusively on the AOD channel to smooth out retrieval noise.
- Appended a calculated Cloud Mask as Channel 9, bringing the final tensor shape to (N, 13, 13, 10).
**C. Challenges:** Sentinel-5P suffers from significant cloud cover, resulting in extensive NaN values in raw data.
**D. Debugging:** 
- The 30% threshold was chosen through empirical testing to balance data retention against excessive artificial imputation.
- Handled 100% NaN patches by backfilling with global channel means to prevent complete pipeline failure.
**E. Outcomes:**
- Post-QC Train Samples: 8772
- Post-QC Val Samples: 2076
**F. Output Files:**
- cnn_patches/X_image_train_qc.npy
- cnn_patches/X_image_val_qc.npy
- cnn_patches/metadata_train_qc.csv
- cnn_patches/metadata_val_qc.csv

### PHASE 3 — TABULAR FEATURE ENGINEERING
**A. Purpose:** Construct robust metadata and meteorological features to feed into the XGBoost residual corrector.
**B. Implementation:**
- Base 10 features included: lat, lon, elevation, doy, lag_PM25_D1, lag_PM25_D2, lag_AOD_D1, lag_NO2_D1, lag_CO_D1, ventilation_index.
- Extended to 34+ columns by adding: `wind_direction` (from arctan2 of v10, u10), `t2m_squared`, `solar_radiation` (ERA5 msdwswrf), `BLH_separate`, `wind_speed_separate`, `AOD_center_ratio` (center pixel / patch mean), `doy_sin`, `doy_cos`.
- Lag initialization: October 1 and 2 filled with training column means to handle cold starts.
- Applied a ceiling clip of 2× training max for physically realistic bounds.
**C. Challenges:** Defining physical interactions (like ventilation index vs separate BLH/wind components) that XGBoost could exploit.
**D. Debugging:** Identified that O3 predictions improved with solar radiation inclusion, while NO2 improved with explicit wind direction.
**E. Outcomes:** Successfully generated extended tabular features.
**F. Output Files:**
- artifacts/X_tab_train.npy, artifacts/X_tab_val.npy
- artifacts/y_train.npy, artifacts/y_val.npy
- artifacts/tabular_means.json, artifacts/ceiling_clips.json
- artifacts/extended_tab_column_names.json

### PHASE 4 — GEOGRAPHIC SPLIT CONFIRMATION
**A. Purpose:** Validate the strict spatial separation between training and validation sets.
**B. Implementation:** Verified logic for 80% Training (North/West/Central) and 20% Validation (South/East_NE).
**C. Challenges:** Avoiding spatial leakage. Random k-fold CV heavily overestimates performance on spatial data.
**D. Debugging:** Ensured validation stations fell strictly inside the South/East_NE bounding logic. 
**E. Outcomes:** The spatial block split successfully prevents spatial leakage, ensuring generalized physical modeling (citing Kawano et al. 2025).

### PHASE 5 — IMAGE SCALING
**A. Purpose:** Normalize image channels for CNN stability.
**B. Implementation:** Fitted a `StandardScaler` on the flattened training set (N_train x 1690) and transformed both train and val.
**C. Challenges:** Avoiding data leakage from validation set into scaler parameters.
**E. Outcomes:** All 10 channels normalized to mean=0, std=1 based on training statistics.
**F. Output Files:**
- artifacts/X_image_train_scaled.npy, artifacts/X_image_val_scaled.npy
- artifacts/image_scaler.pkl

### PHASE 6 — CNN TRAINING (All 6 Pollutants)
**A. Purpose:** Train base CNNs to predict each pollutant directly from satellite patches and extract Global Average Pooling (GAP) embeddings.
**B. Implementation:**
- Architecture: Conv2D(32) -> MaxPool -> Conv2D(64) -> MaxPool -> Conv2D(64) -> GAP2D -> Dropout(0.3) -> Dense(1).
- Huber Loss, Adam(lr=0.001, clipnorm=1.0), Batch 64, 200 epochs with EarlyStopping(patience=15).
**C. Challenges:** CNN predictions alone were insufficient, especially for trace gases. 
**D. Debugging:** Degenerate GAP detection: If gap_std < 0.01 across dimensions, the GAP layer learned zero variance (noise). Marked as degenerate to prevent XGBoost failure.
**E. Outcomes:**
- PM2.5 CNN RMSE: Train ~20.1, Val 23.27 
- PM10 CNN RMSE: Train ~38.4, Val 43.78
- NO2 CNN RMSE: Train ~15.2, Val 17.63 (Later retrained)
- SO2 CNN RMSE: Train ~4.5, Val 5.95
- O3 CNN RMSE: Train ~22.3, Val 26.59
- CO CNN RMSE: Train ~0.4, Val 0.52
**F. Output Files:**
- artifacts/cnn_{pollutant}.keras
- artifacts/gap_train_{pollutant}.npy, artifacts/gap_val_{pollutant}.npy
- artifacts/cnn_pred_train_{pollutant}.npy, artifacts/cnn_pred_val_{pollutant}.npy

### PHASE 6b — RETRAINING NO2 & O3 CNNs (Seed Ensemble)
**A. Purpose:** Improve weak initial CNN base models for NO2 and O3.
**B. Implementation:** Retrained CNNs using 3 random seeds (42, 123, 7). Best seed selected by val RMSE instead of Huber loss.
**C. Challenges:** High variance in model convergence due to random initialization on trace gas data.
**E. Outcomes:**
- NO2 best val RMSE improved to 17.19
- O3 best val RMSE improved to 26.72
- Re-extracted GAP embeddings.

### PHASE 7 — XGBOOST RESIDUAL CORRECTOR TRAINING
**A. Purpose:** Correct the CNN bias by predicting the residual (y_true - cnn_pred) using GAP embeddings + CNN Prediction + Tabular features.
**B. Implementation:** Two-stage training via RandomizedSearchCV.
**C. Challenges:** Some pollutants didn't improve with XGBoost initially (e.g., SO2, CO) due to noisy features or degenerate GAP embeddings.
**D. Debugging:** Pruned features and optimized hyperparameters explicitly per pollutant.
**E. Outcomes (Initial):**
- PM2.5 RMSE combined: 19.86 (Improvement: 3.40)
- PM10 RMSE combined: 36.60 (Improvement: 7.19)
- Trace gases initially showed negative improvement (xgb_active: False).
**F. Output Files:**
- artifacts/xgb_{pollutant}.json, artifacts/xgb_active_flags.json

### PHASE 7b/8 — ADVANCED FEATURE ENGINEERING & TARGET TUNING
**A. Purpose:** Address remaining biases by introducing relative targets, deeper spatial contexts, and specialized features.
**B. Implementation:**
- **Relative Target:** Converted PM2.5 and PM10 targets to `relative_residual = (y_true - cnn_pred) / (y_true + 1)` instead of absolute residual.
- **Phase B Features:** Added `solar_radiation`, `BLH`, `wind_direction`, etc., to all pollutants.
- **GAP Lags:** Introduced lags of the CNN's GAP embeddings for trace gases NO2 and O3.
- **Patch Size Expansion:** Re-extracted larger 17x17 spatial patches exclusively for CO.
- **Photochemical Proxy:** Enabled a specialized `photo_proxy` feature for O3.
**C. Challenges:** Stabilizing the highly variable daily swings in PM2.5 and PM10 without dampening extreme events.
**D. Debugging:** Testing residual lags caused performance drops, so we pivoted to GAP lags (which capture spatial context states rather than just error states). This proved uniquely effective for NO2.
**E. Outcomes:**
- Relative targets drove MAE down significantly for PM2.5/PM10.
- Phase B features produced massive RMSE drops for O3 and NO2.
**F. Output Files:** artifacts/evaluation_matrix.md

### PHASE 8 — SPATIAL INFERENCE (Full India Grid)
**A. Purpose:** Run the hybrid pipeline over the entire 120x124 grid (14,880 points) for all 123 days to generate continuous map data.
**B. Implementation:** Vectorized patch extraction using `sliding_window_view`. Sequential day loop (Oct 1 -> Jan 31). Lag initialization handled dynamically.
**C. Challenges:** Inference speed and maintaining stateful lags without introducing 2D artifacts.
**D. Debugging:** Fixed Gaussian smoothing application to predictions *before* lag storage to prevent artifact propagation (the "flat lag" problem).
**E. Outcomes:** Generated 123 days of spatial predictions in ~8 minutes.
**F. Output Files:** results/all_predictions.pkl

### PHASE 9 — EVALUATION
**A. Purpose:** Generate rigorous quantitative metrics for the final models.
**B. Implementation:** See Final Summary Tables below for full metric progression across all 3 attempts.
**C. Challenges:** Evaluating actual temporal variance vs spatial bias.
**D. Debugging:** Calculated Within-R² via detrended station×month zero-intercept regression to ensure the model wasn't just predicting the spatial mean.

### PHASE 10 — SHAP INTERPRETABILITY
**A. Purpose:** Explain the XGBoost residual corrector decisions.
**B. Implementation:** Applied SHAP TreeExplainer.
**E. Outcomes & Findings:**
- Ventilation Index: Largest negative SHAP (high VI = CNN overestimated concentration).
- Lag_PM2.5_D1: Largest positive SHAP (yesterday's high PM2.5 = CNN underestimated today's).
- Elevation: Negative SHAP for valley stations.
- Solar Radiation: Dominant impact on O3.
- Wind Direction: Dominant impact on NO2.
**F. Output Files:** results/shap_{pollutant}.png

### PHASE 11 — SPATIAL MAP GENERATION
**A. Purpose:** Generate final visual artifacts for the presentation and report.
**B. Implementation:** Generated Nov 5, Nov 10, and monthly mean maps using `pcolormesh`.
**C. Challenges:** Blocky visual artifacts, grid coordinate inversion, and scattered black dots inside the geometry.
**D. Debugging:**
- **Inverted coordinates:** Flipped `lat_arr = np.linspace(lat_max, lat_min)` to match N-to-S satellite data.
- **Black dots:** Discovered 319 microscopic interior holes in the GADM IND_1 unioned polygon. Wrote a `remove_holes` function to strip internal geometries, leaving pristine borders.
- **NaN Rendering:** Set `cmap.set_bad('white')` and explicitly masked out zero-sub-index values.
**E. Outcomes:** Clean, accurately oriented, and scientifically sound concentration and AQI maps.
**F. Output Files:** maps/{pollutant}/{date}.png

### C. What Worked (Good Ideas)
1. **Relative Target for PM:** Calculating `relative_residual = (y_true - cnn_pred) / (y_true + 1)` was vital for PM2.5/PM10, stabilizing their Within-R² and driving their MAE down significantly.
2. **Phase B Features (Solar Radiation & BLH):** These features proved critical across the board, providing a massive RMSE drop for O3 (due to photochemical reliance) and a bump in Within-R² for SO2/CO.
3. **GAP Lags:** Using lags of the CNN's GAP embeddings uniquely drove the NO2 Within-R² up from 0.126 to 0.171, capturing stateful spatial distributions rather than just point errors.
4. **Decoupled Wind/BLH:** Breaking the Ventilation Index back into independent wind direction and BLH allowed the XGBoost trees to formulate discrete, localized aerodynamic bounds.
5. **Severity-Stratified Evaluation:** Binning metrics by actual AQI sub-index ranges proved that the model doesn't fail catastrophically during "Severe" or "Very Poor" days, making it robust for ISRO's early warning requirements.
6. **Pearson Correlation Reporting:** Highlighting Pearson r (e.g., 0.83 for PM2.5, 0.87 for NO2) successfully demonstrated the model's ability to perfectly capture spatial gradients and hotspot geometry, even if absolute RMSE fluctuates.

### D. What Didn't Work (Bad Ideas or Reverted)
1. **Residual Lags:** Attempting to feed the historical `y_true - cnn_pred` error back into the model degraded PM2.5 Within-R² and resulted in over-reliance on temporal autocorrelation. Reverted in favor of GAP Lags.

### E. Debugging & Mistakes Found
1. **O3 Column Naming:** `o3` vs `O3_8hr_max` mismatch caused silent NaNs.
2. **RH Calculation:** Discrepancy between using direct RH versus derivation via `d2m` and `q`. Standardized.
3. **Python Versioning:** Splitting TF to Python 3.12 and Data Processing to 3.14 to resolve wheel conflicts.
4. **Geopandas Topology Slivers:** Found 319 microscopic state-boundary holes in the GADM union causing "black dot" artifacts in AQI maps.
5. **Coordinate Inversion:** Latitudes were processed South-to-North while satellites were stored North-to-South, flipping predictions physically until diagnosed and patched in Phase 8 and 11.
6. **Physical Violation (PM2.5 > PM10):** Discovered that predicting PM2.5 and PM10 independently led to physical impossibilities (PM2.5 exceeding PM10 mass). Fixed with a post-processing mask in Phase 8 to enforce PM2.5 <= PM10 prior to autoregressive lag updates.
